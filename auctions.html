Parachain Slot
Auctions

The key to Kusama’s scalable multichain architecture, parachains connect to Kusama by leasing a slot on the Relay Chain for a period of up to 24 weeks. Leases are granted according to an unpermissioned auction process. As Polkadot’s canary network, parachain auctions will roll out first on Kusama for testing and optimization. Parachains will likely be onboarded to Kusama gradually at first to make sure everything is running smoothly, but no promises.

Expect Chaos

How do teams participate?

The path to launching a parachain on Kusama will differ from team to team, but a typical process could look something like this:

1. Determine a parachain design strategy and BUIDL
2. Test and optimize locally, then on a testnet like Rococo
3. Determine a slot lease auction strategy
4. Join an auction and bid away, for up to approximately 2 weeks
5. Deploy on the acquired slot after the start of the lease period
6. Renew at end of the lease period by joining another auction

Auction mechanics

Parachain slot auctions follow a modified candle auction format, where the exact endpoint of the auction is unknown by participants in order to prevent auction sniping (the strategy of bidding at the last moment) for more accurate price discovery.

1
Teams bid by specifying a contiguous range of 1-4 six-week lease periods and the amount of KSM they are willing to lock up for the duration of the chosen range.
2
Open bidding continues for the duration of the auction. Teams can see other bids and increase their bid over time to stay competitive throughout the auction.
3
At the close of the open bidding period, the precise moment of the auction’s close is randomly determined by a verifiable random function (VRF).
4
The KSM amount bid by the winning team(s) remains locked for the duration of the lease period, after which can be unlocked. Teams can deploy their chain at the start of the lease period.
5
Teams can extend their lease beyond 24 weeks by winning another auction before the conclusion of their current slot lease period.

Slot Crowdloans

Some teams may choose to crowdfund their slot lease using Kusama’s built-in crowdloan mechanism, which allows people to contribute by agreeing to lock up their own KSM until the end of the lease period. Teams can reward their contributors however they see fit, and can structure their crowdloan in various ways, hosting it either natively on Kusama or on a 3rd-party platform.

The typical process for a crowdloan conducted natively on Kusama will look something like this:

1
The team deposits 10 KSM to create a crowdloan campaign, setting a maximum of total allowed contributions and a campaign end date (to account for the possibility of losing one or more auctions, a campaign can last for several auctions), and invites their community to participate.
2
Crowdloan contributors initiate a special transaction allocating transferable DOT to the campaign’s index (this is different from a normal send transaction, contributors should NOT send DOT to an external account).
3
Crowdloan contributors initiate a special transaction allocating transferable KSM to the campaign’s index (this is different from a normal send transaction, contributors should NOT send KSM to an external account).
4
If there are enough contributions to win an auction, the full amount of KSM contributed to the campaign index will remain locked for the duration of the lease period and the chain can be deployed at the start of the lease period.
5
Crowdloaned KSM can be unlocked and returned to contributor’s control at the end of the lease period. In the event that a crowdloan campaign ends without the team winning an auction, contributors’ KSM can be unlocked at the end of the campaign.
